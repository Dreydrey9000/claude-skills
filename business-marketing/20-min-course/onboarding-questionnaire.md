# Client Onboarding Questionnaire

## Source
Ryan Magin's Viral Editz onboarding process. Generated from 7 real onboarding calls. This is the actual questionnaire used before filming to discover content buckets, personality, brand boundaries, and topic opportunities.

## How This Fits the 20-Min-Course Skill

This questionnaire feeds **Layer 1 (Topic Selection)** of the 3-layer content system. It captures WHAT to talk about. Combined with the Voice Capture additions below, it also feeds **Layer 3 (Delivery)** — HOW the person talks.

**Run this BEFORE any content generation mode.** Without it, outputs are generic.

---

## Call Setup

**Purpose:** Pre-content discovery to understand goals, personality, brand boundaries, and content opportunities

**Duration:** 60-90 minutes

**Recording:** ALWAYS record. The recording is the most valuable output — it contains the actual voice data for AI voice profiling.

**Instructions to the person:**
> "This is recorded — I'll send you the recording afterward. I'm going to ask a lot of questions like I'm interviewing you. I'm trying to find buckets of things we can work from for content.
>
> The hard part: when I ask questions, don't give me too much detail. Save the stories for camera. I want natural responses when we're filming. So if I ask 'Do you want your kids to go to private school?' — just yes or no, maybe a quick why, but don't go deep."

**Post-call:** Transcribe with mlx_whisper. Categorize responses. Build voice profile.

## SECTION 1: BUSINESS & REVENUE MODEL

**Primary Questions:**

1. "What do you currently sell? What's your core offer/service?"
2. "What does that cost? What's the price point?"
3. "How do you currently make money? What's your revenue model?"
4. "What's your current revenue? (Monthly/yearly)"
5. "How many employees do you have? What's your monthly payroll?"
6. "Who's your ideal client? What's your target demographic?"

**Follow-up Probes:**
- "What are the commonalities of your best clients?" (cars they drive, income level, lifestyle)
- "What's your current posting cadence?"
- "What aren't you happy with in your current content?"

**Why this matters:** Revenue model determines product ladder positioning. Ideal client description feeds directly into psychographic mapping for Layer 1 topics.

---

## SECTION 2: GOALS & EXPECTATIONS

**Primary Questions:**

1. "If this goes incredibly well and you get millions of views, what does success look like to you?"
2. "What do you want to be known for?"
3. "What DON'T you want to be known for? Any no-go topics?"
4. "If you could switch Instagram accounts with anyone, who would it be?"

**Follow-up Probes:**
- "What's your follower goal by end of year?"
- "Are you looking for direct revenue from content or brand building?"
- "What platforms are priorities?" (Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, Facebook)

**Why this matters:** "What do you want to be known for" = brand positioning. "What DON'T you want" = guardrails for content generation. Aspirational accounts = reference for format and delivery style.

---

## SECTION 3: PERSONAL BRAND & LIFESTYLE

**Primary Questions:**

1. "Brag on yourself for a minute — what are the cool things about you that most people don't know?"
2. "What's weird about you? What quirks do you have that make you unique?"
3. "What are your hobbies and interests outside of business?"
4. "What are some strong opinions you have that might piss people off?"

**Lifestyle Deep-Dive:**
- **Travel:** "Where have you traveled? How do you prefer to travel?" (first class, hotels, etc.)
- **Cars:** "What do you drive? Any strong opinions on vehicles?"
- **Home:** "Tell me about where you live" (flex without flexing)
- **Spending:** "What do you spend money on that others might consider frivolous?"

**Why this matters:** Every answer here is a top-of-funnel content bucket. The psychographic map in Layer 1 IS this section. These are the topics that get the person recommended to their ideal buyer's feed.

---

## SECTION 4: FAMILY & RELATIONSHIPS

**Primary Questions:**

1. "Are you married? Kids? Tell me about your family structure"
2. "Any strong parenting beliefs or non-negotiables?"
3. "What type of schooling for your kids?" (public, private, homeschool)
4. "What are some things your spouse/family would say are weird about you?"

**Relationship Dynamics:**
- "Who makes more money in the relationship?"
- "Any relationship advice or strong opinions on marriage/partnerships?"
- "How do you handle work-life balance with family?"

**Why this matters:** Family content is top-of-funnel psychographic gold. If the ideal buyer is married with kids, family content gets recommended to their feed. The "weird about you" question is where the best humanizing content lives.

---

## SECTION 5: VALUES & BELIEFS

**Primary Questions:**

1. "What are your core values that you won't compromise on?"
2. "Any religious or spiritual beliefs that influence your brand?"
3. "What's your money philosophy? Any strong financial beliefs?"
4. "What advice would you give to your younger self?"

**Controversial Territory:**
- "Any political opinions you're comfortable sharing?"
- "What's something you believe that most people disagree with?"
- "What industry practices do you think are bullshit?"

**Why this matters:** This is the raw material for POV content and "us vs them" rally posts (Format 3). Strong beliefs = strong content. Neutral = invisible.

---

## SECTION 6: HEALTH & WELLNESS

**Primary Questions:**

1. "What's your fitness routine? Are you into biohacking?"
2. "Any extreme health practices or strong opinions on wellness?"
3. "What are your vices? Where do you 'fail' as a person?"

**Biohacking Deep-Dive:**
- "Cold plunge, sauna, red light therapy?"
- "Supplements, TRT, any medical optimization?"
- "Strong opinions on diet, alcohol, caffeine?"

**Why this matters:** Health and wellness content performs across every niche. The "vices" question humanizes — showing imperfection builds trust faster than showing perfection.

---

## SECTION 7: CONTENT HISTORY & PREFERENCES

**Primary Questions:**

1. "What content do you currently consume? Who do you follow?"
2. "What's not working with your current content strategy?"
3. "What parts of your personality aren't being shown in your content?"
4. "Any content you've made that you're proud of or that performed well?"

**Why this matters:** Past successes show what already works. "Parts of personality not shown" = untapped content veins. Consumption habits = taste reference for delivery style.

---

## SECTION 8: LOGISTICS & PRACTICAL

**Primary Questions:**

1. "Where are you located?"
2. "Do you have someone who can help with filming/be your videographer?"
3. "What locations do we have access to for filming?" (home, office, other properties)
4. "How many outfit changes can you do?"

## ADVANCED PROBING TECHNIQUES

### The "Annoying Kid Framework"
Keep asking "why" until you get to elementary school level explanations.
- Push for specific details: "What time do you wake up? What's the first thing you do?"
- Get tactical: "What brand of pen do you use? Why that one specifically?"

### Controversy Mining
- "What would your competitors say about you that would piss you off?"
- "What industry 'best practice' do you think is complete bullshit?"
- "What's something successful people do that you think is stupid?"

### Lifestyle Flexing (Without Being Obvious)
- "What's your monthly credit card points balance?"
- "What's the most you've ever spent on a single purchase?"
- "What's something you buy that most people would consider expensive?"

---

## RED FLAGS & BOUNDARIES

**Client Red Flags:**
- Won't share any personal information
- Everything is "off limits" for content
- Wants to script everything in advance
- Afraid of any controversy or strong opinions
- Only wants to talk about their product/service

**Boundary Setting:**
> "We need to find your authentic personality — if everything is sanitized, it won't work"
> "The goal is controlled controversy, not chaos"
> "We can always delete something, but we can't create passion that isn't there"

## VOICE CAPTURE ADDITIONS (For AI Voice Profiling)

These questions are NOT in the original onboarding call. They are added specifically so that AI-generated content sounds like the actual person, not like a template.

**Run these either during the call or as a separate 15-minute voice recording.**

### Speech & Vocabulary
1. "Do you swear in your content? How much? What words?"
2. "When you explain something, do you use analogies? What kind?" (sports, food, movies, business)
3. "Are you more hype/high-energy or calm/authoritative on camera?"
4. "What's a phrase you catch yourself saying all the time?"

### Stories Bank
5. "What's the story you tell most often when you're trying to make a point?"
6. "What's your go-to 'humble brag' — the thing you casually mention that's actually impressive?"
7. "What's a failure you're comfortable talking about publicly?"
8. "What's the most unexpected result a client got?"

### Anti-Patterns (Guardrails)
9. "What kind of content makes you cringe when you see others do it?"
10. "What words or phrases would you NEVER use?" (e.g., "leveraging synergies," "crushing it," etc.)
11. "What's a compliment about your content that would actually mean something to you?"
12. "If someone wrote a post pretending to be you and got it wrong, what would give it away?"

### Buyer Language
13. "What exact words do your customers use when they describe their problem? Not your words — theirs."
14. "What's the #1 DM or comment you get over and over?"
15. "Read me a recent DM from someone who bought from you — what did they say?"

## POST-CALL PROCESS

### Immediate Actions (Within 2 Hours)
1. Send recording to client
2. Transcribe with mlx_whisper (word timestamps enabled)
3. Run through Poppy AI (or manual categorization)
4. Create topic list from responses

### Topic Categorization
- **Hobbies & Interests** — broad reach, top-of-funnel content
- **Expertise** — things they've made money from but aren't their main business
- **Mastery** — core business/expertise, middle and bottom of funnel
- **Personal/Lifestyle** — family, relationships, opinions
- **Controversial Takes** — strong opinions, hot buttons, rally content

### Voice Profile Build
From the recording + voice capture additions, extract:
- Vocabulary patterns (words they use / words they avoid)
- Sentence length and structure
- Energy level and cadence
- Recurring stories (indexed)
- Hot takes and strong opinions
- Analogies and metaphors they naturally use
- Humor style
- Profanity level and style
- "Sounds like them" example quotes
- "Would never say this" guardrails
- Buyer language from DMs/comments

**Output:** `voice-profile-[name].md` — referenced by all content generation modes in the 20-min-course skill.

## CONTENT GOLDMINE SIGNALS

Listen for these during the call — they indicate high-performing content topics:

- "People always tell me I'm weird because..."
- "I have a strong opinion about..."
- "My family thinks it's crazy that I..."
- "I spend way too much money on..."
- "I never talk about this publicly, but..."
- "What pisses me off about my industry is..."
- "Everyone does X but I think that's stupid because..."
