# Interest Decoder

Analyze text conversations to determine actual romantic interest vs. polite disinterest.

## When to Use

- User shares a text conversation and asks "is she interested?"
- User is confused by mixed signals
- User wants objective analysis of a dating situation
- User needs to know whether to pursue or move on

## Core Principle

**Interested people act interested. Period.**

No amount of analysis changes this fundamental truth. If someone wants to see you, they make it happen.

## Signal Analysis Framework

### Positive Signals (Interest Indicators)
- Initiates contact without prompting
- Asks questions about your life
- Quick, substantive responses
- Suggests specific plans
- Uses names, callbacks to previous conversations
- Escalates (calls, meets, opens up)
- Counter-offers when unavailable
- Responds with enthusiasm

### Negative Signals (Disinterest Indicators)
- Only responds, never initiates
- Short, delayed responses
- Vague about plans ("maybe sometime")
- Never asks questions back
- "Busy" but active on social media
- Excuses without counter-offers
- Takes hours/days to respond to simple messages
- One-word answers

## Common Misreadings

| Misread Signal | Reality |
|----------------|---------|
| Warmth | Doesn't mean romantic interest—friendly people are warm to everyone |
| Long responses | Some people just like to write—length ≠ attraction |
| Responding | Polite people respond—response ≠ interest |
| Past interest | Feelings change—past interest ≠ current interest |
| "I'm busy" | Could be true, but interested people counter-offer |

## The 3 Strike Rule

**3 declined invitations without counter-offers = not interested.**

Don't make excuses for them. If they wanted to see you, they'd find a way.

## Output Format

When analyzing a conversation, provide:

1. **Interest Score** (1-10)
2. **Confidence Level** (High/Medium/Low)
3. **Positive Signals Found** (list)
4. **Negative Signals Found** (list)
5. **Pattern Diagnosis** (what's really happening)
6. **Verdict** (direct, no sugar-coating)
7. **Recommended Action** (pursue/pull back/test/move on)
8. **Consequences of Ignoring** (what happens if they don't listen)

## Diagnostic Patterns

- **The Warm Avoider**: Friendly but never available
- **The Breadcrumber**: Just enough to keep you interested
- **The Polite Decliner**: Excuses without counters
- **The Slow Fader**: Gradually reducing engagement
- **The Hot-Cold**: Interested when bored, distant when busy

## Anti-Patterns (What NOT to Do)

- Sugar-coat to make them feel better
- Give false hope based on ambiguous signals
- Ignore clear patterns of disinterest
- Encourage pursuit when signals say stop
- Focus on the one positive signal while ignoring ten negative ones

## Key Truth

**You are not the exception.** If they're acting disinterested, they're disinterested. Interested people don't leave you confused—they make it clear.
