### PACING AND LEADING

The foundation of all cold reading. Pace her reality first (say true things about her), THEN lead her where you want.

**Example:** "So you're a brown-eyed, tan-skinned 26-year-old single female in the big starry-eyed city of Los Angeles chasing your dream. It's not an easy road, is it? I know it's kind of a deep thing to say, but I bet you've asked yourself at one point or another -- why am I really out here doing this? I'm curious, what was your answer?"

- Bold = pacing (all obviously true things you observed)
- Italics = leading (where you actually wanted to go)
- She gives a DEEPER answer because you paced her first. Without pacing, she gives you a bullshit surface answer.
- Can also be physical: pace emotionally first, then "Hey, I have an idea, let's go" + grab her hand

### 6 KEY PRINCIPLES FOR ALL COLD READS

**1. Always Use Vague Language**
- "I don't know why, but..." / "one time or another" / "I feel like maybe..."
- Vague = leeway to be right. Specific = easy to be wrong
- BAD: "You watch SportsCenter at 9 AM every morning"
- GOOD: "I get this weird feeling you're one of those girls who actually enjoys SportsCenter"

**2. Break Her Into Parts & Sides**
- "There's a part of you that loves to party... but there's also this part that just wants to chill on a Friday night"
- When you use "sometimes this, sometimes that" -- she CANNOT disagree because it covers both ends
- "You have a side everyone sees... but I can also tell there's this other side..."

**3. Masculine Certainty / Frame Control (MOST IMPORTANT)**
- 100% belief in what you're saying. You are obviously intuitive. This is obviously true.
- Playful but certain. Not dictator energy -- relaxed dominance.
- **If she disagrees:** Offer ZERO resistance. Bruce Lee verbal judo. "OK, awesome." Then pattern interrupt + lead elsewhere.
- "Damn! I don't know, I'm just really thirsty. Let's get a drink." = breaks the pattern, she forgets the disagreement
- **She doesn't care what the frame IS. She cares that it's impenetrable.**

**4. Deep But Relaxed Eye Contact**
- Strong, powerful, but relaxed. Slight squint -- like you're watching a mystery movie unfold
- "Rubbing your chin" energy. Little playful smirk.
- You're reading her. She can feel it.

**5. Include Your Reason Why**
- BAD: "You're totally a bad girl."
- GOOD: "It's the way you make these evil little smiles every so often -- I can just tell there's this side to you..."
- Even "I don't know why, but..." works. Just acknowledging there IS a reason makes it stick harder.

**6. Data vs. Process**
- When talking about deep topics (especially sex), discuss PROCESS not DATA
- DATA = how many partners, what age she lost virginity (boring, judgmental)
- PROCESS = "What was it like? How did it feel? What was your favorite part? What were you thinking?"
- Women connect through process. Men default to data. Switch to process.

### TYPE 1: SNAP COLD READS (First 10-60 Seconds)

Quick attraction spikes. Use early, use often, pepper throughout.

**4 Main Structures:**

**Structure 1: "You seem like a..."**
- "I don't know why, but you seem like a nurse."
- If right = you're amazing. If wrong = she volunteers real info ("I'm actually a teacher") without you asking
- She thinks: "I'm sharing information with him quickly, he must be cool"
- Use anything: nurse, teacher, yoga girl, artist, poet, marathon runner, someone who's gone bull fighting

**Structure 2: "You're an X, aren't you?"**
- "You're a really sarcastic girl, aren't you?"
- Even if she hasn't been sarcastic, masculine certainty makes it work
- Right or wrong doesn't matter. The frame control behind it is what matters.

**Structure 3: "You strike me as..."**
- "You strike me as a girl who played soccer in high school"
- "You strike me as a girl who thought grades were super important in high school, then got to college and realized partying was way more fun"
- Playful, challenging, fun emotions. Great conversational igniters.

**Structure 4: "I feel like you..."**
- "I feel like you're a girl who loves to travel the globe"
- She CANNOT argue with how you FEEL. Women are aligned with "feelings = truth"
- If she asks why, give a little bit, make her work for more, give a little, make her chase

**Snap Cold Read → Conversation Example:**
You're bullshitting with her for a few minutes, then pause: "You're not a bullshitter, are you?" She says no. You go side by side, look around the venue: "Look at everyone here. 95% of these people are just pure bullshit. They're telling each other what they want to hear. Life is just too fucking short for bullshit." Now you have serious common ground + status attraction → take it anywhere.

### COLD READ LABELING (Evolution of Snap Cold Reads)

**Principle:** When you give something a LABEL, you gain a sense of power and control over it. She feels it subconsciously.

**The "Under-the-Oath Face" Example:**
- Instead of: "I feel like you're really good at lying" (decent snap cold read)
- Say: "Did you know you have the most intense under-the-oath face I think I've ever seen?"
- 100% of the time she asks: "What's an under-the-oath face? What does that mean?"
- There IS no such thing. Jason made it up. But the LABEL makes it feel 10x more important and real.
- Then explain: "It's this face you make sometimes... I can just tell, you're probably really good at lying"

**Create Your Own Labels:**
- "You're such an oak tree girl" → strong, independent, grounded, sturdy on her own
- "You're totally a sunflower girl" → spiritual, happy-go-lucky, enjoying life
- "You're such a wine girl" → sophisticated but old soul energy

**Label → Theme Conversations:**
For each label, map out conversational themes. "Oak tree girl" → masculine vs feminine energy, why she became so strong, goals, people you've known like her. Plan where you'd take the conversation.

Take the label you just gave and disqualify her with it:

### TYPE 2: DEEPER COLD READS (Qualifying & Connecting)

**The Commonalities Exercise:**
Make a list: What commonalities do attractive, in-demand girls share? Then extrapolate those to what it means about her life, how people treat her, how she sees the world.

Jason's examples:
- Seriously into fashion (even "I don't care about fashion" girls care)
- Go on Facebook to look at their OWN posts/photos, not stalk others
- Annoyed by people judging them only on looks

**The "Sometimes This, Sometimes That" Formula:**
- "Sometimes you're the center of attention, the ball of energy everyone feeds off. But sometimes you also like to sit back, observe, people-watch, and just chill."
- She can't disagree because it covers both sides with vague language
- **Pro tip:** Create the two parts first, then lock in SPECIFIC DETAILS inside each part. Details inside the structure make it sound much more true.

**The Guessing Game:**
After 10+ minutes of connection, say: "I can tell you're a really intuitive person just by the way you talk and communicate. You probably get a lot of impulses that later turn out to be true. You know what? We need to play the guessing game right now."

Rules: All guesses must be personality-based or experience-based. No boring data (where you were born, what college). I make a guess, you make a guess.

**Why it's powerful:** The game is a SHOWCASE for your cold reading ability. You make 3-4 cold reads in a row and she thinks "God, this guy fucking gets me."

- When she gets one wrong: "You're never going to get me on a second date with that kind of subpar showing" (implies a first date will happen)
- When she gets one right: Stare her down, let tension build, smirk, pull her in for a big dominant bear hug, whisper "God, you just get me." Then toss her off and go back to the game.

### The 5 Techniques Applied to Women (from Email Income Experts)

**1. Problem-Agitate-Diagnose**
Describe her situation, agitate it, then diagnose:
- Problem: She keeps attracting the wrong guys
- Agitate: What if this never changes? What if 5 years from now...
- Diagnose: "The reason is..." (paradigm shift)
- Did you teach anything? No. But she now thinks "He's got something."

**2. "Me Too" Stories**
Tell stories that make her mentally raise her hand:
- Reference shared experiences from the Customer Prototype
- Use the 4 Forces (Frustrations, Fears, Wants, Aspirations) to hit universal experiences
- "The more personal, the more universal"

**3. Cold Reads**
General statements that create instant connection:
- "You're the type who doesn't let people in easily"
- "You've been hurt before but you'd never admit it"
- "You pretend not to care but you actually care way too much"

**4. The Enemy & The Fight**
Identify a shared frustration and create an "us vs them":
- "Isn't it frustrating when guys [common bad behavior]?"
- Name the enemy (the cause of the pain)
- Position yourself as the exception

**5. Overt Statements**
Simple but powerful:
- "I know what it's like"
- "I get it"
- "I know how frustrating that can be"
- "I know how scary that can be"
- These overtly tell her you understand what it's like to be her
