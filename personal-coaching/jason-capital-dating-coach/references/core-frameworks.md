## NAMED ATTRACTION TECHNIQUES

### 1. Brain Scramblers
Contradictory statements where actions oppose words, delivered with a smirk:
- "Hi, I'm [Name]. I'm shy." (while displaying confidence)
- "You're so funny, stop making me laugh"
- "You're far too tall for me"
- **Mechanism:** Creates cognitive dissonance while displaying supreme indifference

### 2. Past Adventure Projections (PAPs)
Fabricated shared memories presented as fact:
- "Remember our first night here? You asked for a kid's menu"
- "Remember when we went to summer camp together?"
- **Mechanism:** Emotional brain cannot distinguish imagined from real memories

### 3. Future Adventure Projections (FAPs)
Detailed narratives about future activities together:
- "When we go swimming with dolphins together..."
- Add absurd details (encountering mountain lions you can "communicate" with)
- **Mechanism:** Creates emotional bonding through imagined shared experiences

### 4. Teasing Questions
Playful inquiries implying desirable qualities:
- "So how long have you been attracted to me now?"
- "What is it you find so intriguing about me, besides my humility?"
- "Isn't it fitting that the two coolest people here are talking?"

### 5. False Disqualifiers
Fake reasons for incompatibility:
- "It's too bad you're not my type"
- "We'd never work out"
- "I'm suspicious of you"
- "You're totally a bad girl pretending to be good"
- **Mechanism:** Humans pursue what they cannot have

### 6. Ridiculous Requests
Absurd demands made half-seriously:
- "Go get me groceries and make a sandwich"
- "After we divorce and remarry, will you still love me?"
- **Mechanism:** Subconsciously communicates you deserve extraordinary treatment

### 7. Blaming Her
- "Stop being so cool and making me like you"
- "Stop looking at me like that -- it's too seductive"
- "You know I have a weakness for women skilled in movement"
- **Mechanism:** Sets frame that SHE is seducing HIM

### 8. Role-Plays
Jason describes these as "like crack for them":
- **Boyfriend/Girlfriend:** "This is my girlfriend for the next 10 minutes" + simulated breakups/reconciliations
- **Partners in Crime:** Detective work -- you're Sherlock, she's Watson
- **Teacher/Student:** Use any knowledge-sharing moment as instructional dynamic

- **Key:** Don't make a big deal. Laugh it off. Start talking about something else.
