### Content Formats That Work
1. **Contrarian parenting opinions** — bold stance against common advice (3.6M, 2.6M)
2. **Practical hacks with specifics** — exact products, numbers, routines (2.1M, 1.1M)
3. **"Here's what we do" demonstrations** — show don't tell (396K-879K)
4. **Dad-energy forward** — he leads the take, she validates/adds (consistent pattern)
5. **Vivid examples** — named brands (Depeche Mode Radio, not "some music"), exact numbers (3 songs, 10 minutes)

### Optimal Length
- **Under 60 seconds** for highest performers
- 45-60s sweet spot for hack/tip content
- Can go 90s+ only for strong story-based content

### Visual Style
- Bold yellow-on-black captions (their brand)
- Fast cuts between speakers
- Illustrative images/product shots when referencing specific things
- Clean, well-lit talking head (side-by-side split screen)

### Voice & Tone
- Direct, no hedging ("MY RULE IS" not "I think maybe...")
- Specific over vague ("Depeche Mode Radio" not "some 80s music")
- Confident dad energy — teaching from experience, not theory
- Wife adds credibility/relatability ("that's one of the gifts you taught me")
- Conversational but punchy — no filler words

### What NOT To Do
- Don't start soft ("So one of our favorite...") — start BOLD
- Don't explain too much context before the hook
- Don't include wife's extended elaboration if it repeats the point
- Don't go over 60s for hack content
- Don't use generic descriptions — always name specific things

### Clipping Strategy
1. **Hook first 3-5s** — bold statement or contrarian take
2. **Concept reveal by 8-10s** — name the hack/technique
3. **Specific details 10-35s** — the how, with vivid examples
4. **Payoff/result 35-50s** — what happens when you do this
5. **Optional wife validation 50-60s** — short confirmation, not repetition

### Cutting Rules (Learned from Editor Cuts)

**Studied from:** "Adding music to chores" — editor produced 2 versions (50s tight, 75s full)

#### What to Cut
- **Filler openers** — "so a lot of times" → just start the sentence ("we have a playroom")
- **Redundant detail loops** — if they say the same thing twice with different words, keep only the punchier version. Example: "on one of the stations or the apps there. And so I just click it on Depeche Mode Radio and it starts cranking" → cut entirely, just keep "Depeche Mode Radio setup" and jump to action
- **Setup that the viewer can infer** — "where they play and the family kinda hangs out" adds nothing; "playroom downstairs" already paints the picture
- **Breathing room between thoughts** — tighten gaps between sentences, keep momentum

#### What to Keep
- **Named specifics** — "Depeche Mode Radio", "three song cleanup", "10 minutes" — these are the retention hooks
- **Action sequences** — "I start handing them things, they start taking them, before you know it I step out and they just run it" — this is the payoff viewers wait for
- **Emotional energy moments** — "galvanizing energy", "let's get to it" — the dad energy IS the content
- **Wife's closer IF it adds a new insight** — "when we pair fun with boring we become way more effective" is a takeaway, not a repeat. Keep it.

#### Split Test Strategy
- **Version 1 (tight, ~50s):** Hook → concept → specifics → payoff. Ends at the result. No wife section. Tests pure dad-energy punchy format.
- **Version 2 (full, ~75s):** Same as V1 but includes wife's gym analogy + "pair fun with boring" closer. Tests whether the lesson/takeaway ending boosts saves and shares.
- Always deliver BOTH versions for split testing unless told otherwise.

#### Cutting Mindset
- Every sentence must earn its spot — if removing it doesn't lose meaning, cut it
- Cuts should be invisible — jump between natural sentence boundaries, never mid-thought
- When two sentences say the same thing, keep the one with more energy or specificity
- The viewer should never feel a cut happened — if a splice sounds weird, keep the full run instead of forcing a choppy edit
